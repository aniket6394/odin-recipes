Rosogolla is a popular, syrupy dessert from South Asia that's made from chhena dough dumplings cooked in sugar syrup:

Rasgulla Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2-3 tbsp lemon juice or white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp cardamom powder (optional)

Steps:

  1. Make Chhena (Paneer): Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle the milk. Stir gently until the milk curdles, and the whey separates. Strain the curds through a muslin or cheese cloth to collect the chhena (paneer). Rinse it under cold water to remove the sourness. Squeeze out excess water.
  2. Knead the Chhena: Transfer the chhena to a flat surface and knead it for 5-7 minutes until smooth and soft. The dough should be pliable and without lumps. If sticky, grease your hands lightly with ghee or water.
  3. Shape the Rasgullas: Divide the dough into small, smooth balls (about the size of a walnut). Ensure there are no cracks on the surface, as cracks can cause them to break during cooking.
  4. Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a wide pan, add water and sugar, and bring it to a boil. Stir occasionally until the sugar dissolves. Once boiling, reduce heat to medium.
  5. Cook the Rasgullas: Gently drop the shaped rasgullas into the simmering sugar syrup. Cover and cook for 15-20 minutes, ensuring they puff up and become soft. They should double in size. Remove from heat and let them cool in the syrup.
